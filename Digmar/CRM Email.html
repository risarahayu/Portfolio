CRM Email Campaign

Introduction

  • 📌 Bootcamp Assignment: This task was assigned by the bootcamp to practice analyzing CRM processes through email campaigns.
  • 📧 Non-Organic Email: The emails used were provided by the bootcamp, but the content was designed to be as engaging as possible.
  • 🎯 Goal: To practice analyzing CRM processes using email, particularly for customer retention.

Strategy

  • 🔍 A/B Testing: Conducted to evaluate content preferences.
  • 📊 Aspects Evaluated:
    • Educational content vs. straightforward discount offers.
    • The impact of sending days (Wednesday vs. Saturday) on open rates and click rates.

Insights

  • 📤 Open Rate:
    • ✅ Emails sent on Wednesday and Saturday had the same open rate.
  • 🖱️ Click Rate:
    • ✅ Emails sent on Saturday had a 2% higher click rate compared to those sent on Wednesday.
  • 📦 Content Preference:
    • ✅ Content featuring a catalog with discounts received more clicks than content with only a discount banner.

Recommendations

📩 Email Subject Lines:

  • 🌟 Personalization: Example: "Enjoy 50% Off, |FNAME|!"
  • 💡 Focus on Benefits: Example: "The Secret to Clear Skin: 50% Year-End Discount!"
  • ⏳ Urgency: Example: "50% Off Ends December 31, |FNAME|!"

🎯 Audience Segmentation:

  • Focus on audiences with stronger behaviors, such as frequent website visitors or those with a transaction history.

📢 Call-to-Action (CTA):

  • Create more specific CTAs that encourage immediate action, e.g., “Click now before it’s gone!”

Conclusion

  • 📅 Sending Day: Saturday is more effective for increasing click rates.
  • 📂 Content: Catalogs with discounts are more engaging than standalone discount banners.
  • 🚀 Optimization: Personalization, benefit-focused messaging, and specific CTAs can improve email campaign effectiveness.
